BiblIA Project Team

  • BiblIA Dataset Project
    Hebrew OCR & HTR Research Initiative
  • פרויקט מאגר BiblIA
    יוזמת מחקר לזיהוי טקסט עברי

The BiblIA Dataset project focuses on advancing Hebrew OCR and HTR technology through collaborative research and open-source development.

פרויקט מאגר BiblIA מתמקד בקידום טכנולוגיות זיהוי טקסט עברי באמצעות מחקר שיתופי ופיתוח קוד פתוח.

Core Project Team

  • Daniel Stökl Ben Ezra (EPHE‑PSL, UMR AOROC 8546), Co‑Director
  • Peter Stokes (EPHE‑PSL, UMR AOROC 8546), Co‑Director
  • Benjamin Kiessling (EPHE‑PSL, UMR AOROC 8546), kraken Lead Developer
  • Robin Tissot (EPHE‑PSL, UMR AOROC 8546), eScriptorium Lead Developer

The ‘core’ team are the founding members of the eScriptorium project and have led its development and implementation since the beginning. The two developers have worked full-time on kraken and eScriptorium since the beginning, and the two co-directors have been primarily responsible for coordinating the development, contributions and funding applications.

Other Contributors

We have done our best to note the contributors to the eScriptorium software, but if you or anyone you know is missing then do please let us know.

We are also very grateful for other types of contribution that is not specifically related to the software. We cannot list all of these here, but they include the publication of open datasets (such as those on HTR-United), open models (particularly those on the kraken open model repository), tickets reporting bugs and improvements, writing documentation, organising training sessions, producing plugins and other tools to use with kraken and eScriptorium, and so on.
